Lagos State Government Launches Free Coding Program for Primary and Secondary School Students

The Lagos State Government has introduced a free coding program for primary and secondary school students, aimed at equipping them with essential digital skills. This initiative is part of the government's efforts to prepare young Nigerians for the digital age.

Lagos State has launched a free coding program for primary and secondary school students, expanding its long-running CodeLagos initiative to empower young learners with digital skills. The program offers hands-on training in programming, animation, and mobile app development, targeting underserved communities across the state.

Program Overview

  • Initiative Name: CodeLagos / Kids Coding Partnership

  • Target Audience: Primary and Secondary School students in Lagos State (especially underserved communities like Ikorodu and Yaba).

  • Duration: 3-month curriculum (with expansion phases planned).

  • Focus Areas:

    • Basic programming (Scratch, Python, Java)
    • Web development (HTML, CSS)
    • Animation and mobile app creation
    • Emerging technologies (Blockchain, Oracle’s Alice, Greenfoot)

Goals of the Program

  • Bridge the digital divide by reaching children who have little or no access to technology.
  • Empower students with practical coding skills that prepare them for future careers in tech.
  • Create pathways to internships, scholarships, and further education opportunities.
  • Position Lagos as Africa’s technology frontier by training over one million residents in coding.

Partnerships & Support

  • BorderlessTek (UK-based, led by Nigerian tech entrepreneur Wale Atekoja) spearheads the Kids Coding Partnership.
  • Collaborations include:
    • Yesding – education facilitation
    • Proline – internet infrastructure
    • Akowe App – credential verification with internationally recognized digital certificates.
  • The Lagos State Government also partners with NGOs, religious organizations, and libraries to host coding centres.

Why It Matters

  • Future-ready skills: Coding is now considered a core literacy in the digital age.
  • Economic empowerment: Students gain tools to participate in Nigeria’s growing digital economy.
  • Inclusivity: Focus on children who have never touched a laptop ensures no one is left behind.

Next Steps

The program is expected to expand further with hackathons, contests, and more centres across Lagos. Students will not only learn coding but also apply it to solve real-world problems in areas like health, transport, and education.
