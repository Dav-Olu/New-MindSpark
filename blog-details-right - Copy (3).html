FG Launches 'Girls' Education Project' to Boost Enrollment and Retenation Rates

The Federal Government has launched the 'Girls' Education Project' (GEP), a comprehensive initiative aimed at improving access to quality education for girls across Nigeria. This project seeks to address the disparities in educational opportunities and outcomes for girls.

The Federal Government of Nigeria has unveiled the “Girls’ Education Project,” a landmark initiative designed to boost enrollment and retention rates of girls in schools nationwide. This program underscores the government’s commitment to gender equity in education and aligns with global Sustainable Development Goals (SDG 4 & 5).

Project Overview

  • Program Name: Girls’ Education Project (GEP)

  • Target Audience: Girls in primary and secondary schools, especially in underserved rural and northern communities.

  • Core Focus Areas:

    • Increasing school enrollment for girls.
    • Reducing dropout rates through retention strategies.
    • Providing safe, inclusive, and supportive learning environments.
    • Offering scholarships, mentorship, and community sensitization.

Key Objectives

  • Boost enrollment: Ensure more girls enter school at the right age.
  • Retention strategies: Address challenges like early marriage, poverty, and cultural barriers.
  • Teacher training: Equip educators with gender-sensitive teaching methods.
  • Community engagement: Mobilize parents, traditional leaders, and NGOs to support girls’ education.

Partnerships & Support

  • Collaboration with UNICEF and the UK Department for International Development (DFID), building on earlier Girls’ Education Project phases.
  • Local NGOs and women’s advocacy groups will help implement grassroots awareness campaigns.
  • Digital learning platforms will be introduced to expand access for girls in remote areas.

Why It Matters

  • Breaking barriers: Tackles cultural and economic obstacles that keep girls out of school.
  • Economic empowerment: Educated girls are more likely to contribute to Nigeria’s workforce and economy.
  • Health & social benefits: Girls who complete school are less likely to marry early and more likely to raise healthier families.

Next Steps

The government plans to roll out monitoring systems to track progress, expand scholarship opportunities, and integrate digital literacy into the curriculum. The project will also include mentorship programs pairing young girls with female role models in STEM, leadership, and entrepreneurship.
