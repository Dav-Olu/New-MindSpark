FG Launches Intiative to Improve Digital Literacy in Schools.

The Federal Government of Nigeria has unveiled a landmark initiative aimed at transforming the nation’s education system by embedding digital literacy into schools. This move is part of a broader national strategy to prepare Nigerian students for the realities of a technology-driven global economy.

Why Digital Literacy Matters for Nigeria

In today’s world, digital literacy is no longer optional—it is a fundamental skill. For Nigeria, a country with one of the fastest-growing youth populations, ensuring that students can navigate digital tools is critical for:

  • Economic competitiveness: equipping the workforce with skills for jobs in ICT, fintech, and creative industries.

  • Educational advancement: enabling students to access online learning resources and global knowledge.

  • Social inclusion: bridging the gap between urban and rural communities.

Key Features of the Initiative

  • Curriculum Integration: Digital literacy modules will be introduced across primary and secondary schools.

  • Teacher Training: Educators will undergo specialized training to deliver digital skills effectively.

  • Infrastructure Support: Provision of laptops and internet-enabled devices to schools, especially in underserved areas.

  • Certification: Students completing the programme will receive internationally recognized digital skills certificates.

  • Community Outreach: Beyond schools, artisans and informal sector workers will also benefit from tailored digital training.

Impact on Nigerian Schools

This initiative is expected to reshape the classroom experience:

  • Students will learn coding, online safety, and digital entrepreneurship alongside traditional subjects.
  • Teachers will become facilitators of digital learning, moving beyond chalkboards to interactive platforms.
  • Rural schools will gain access to digital tools, reducing the urban-rural education divide.

Strategic Importance

The programme aligns with Nigeria’s National Digital Economy Policy and Strategy and President Bola Tinubu’s Renewed Hope Agenda. By targeting 95% digital literacy by 2030, the government is positioning Nigeria as a hub for digital innovation in Africa.

Challenges Ahead

While the initiative is ambitious, several hurdles remain:

  • Infrastructure gaps: Many rural areas still lack reliable electricity and internet access.
  • Funding sustainability: Ensuring long-term financing beyond initial sponsorships.
  • Awareness and adoption: Encouraging schools, parents, and communities to embrace digital learning.

The Federal Government’s digital literacy initiative is more than an education reform—it is a national transformation strategy. By equipping students with digital skills, Nigeria is laying the foundation for a future where its youth can thrive in the global digital economy.
